WHICH BACTERIA ARE IMPACTING YOUR HEART

The human gut microbiome has been identified as an indicator of cardiovascular health and disease. An aberrant bacterial composition is becoming increasingly crucial for understanding inflammation-induced metabolic syndromes from diabetes to obesity to cardiovascular challenges. The dietary impact of unhealthy foods induces the bacteria to produce/process metabolites like choline, L-carnitine or TMAO urging the heart towards further un-fitness. In contrast, healthy production of SCFAs (acetate, propionate, butyrate) by the bacteria enables the maintenance of the gut barrier, modulates heart risk factors, and regulates glucose and lipid balance.

WHICH BACTERIA ARE IMPACTING YOUR EMOTIONAL WELL-BEING

Millenniums of co-existing with bacteria have given rise to a wonderful lively dialogue between them and us. Prolonged psychological pressure and stress can induce the bacteria to produce neurohormones that alter our mood and behaviour. The reshaping of the gut composition can lead to heightened inflammation and a cyclical situation that feeds itself. Consequently, this can trigger a bloom in pathogens, a leaky gut or results in a variety of metabolic upheavals.

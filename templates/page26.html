WHICH BACTERIA ARE IMPACTING YOUR AGING

The gut microbiome plays a multifaceted role in biological aging. It can influence muscle atrophy by impacting nutrient absorption and inflammation, potentially contributing to age-related muscle loss. An imbalanced microbiome may trigger inflammation and produce neurotoxic compounds, exacerbating conditions like Alzheimer's and Parkinson's disease. Additionally, the microbiome's effects on DNA damage susceptibility are linked to inflammation and oxidative stress. Dysbiosis, characterized by microbial imbalance, can disrupt the gut barrier, allowing harmful substances to enter circulation, promoting inflammation and accelerating aging. Alterations in nutrient sensing pathways, influenced by the microbiome, can lead to metabolic dysregulation and age-related diseases. Lastly, a dysbiotic microbiome can foster chronic inflammation, a key driver of various age-related conditions, further emphasizing the microbiome's crucial role in the aging process.

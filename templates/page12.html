BACTERIA INVOLVED IN AFFECTING BLOOD PRESSURE

The gut and its inhabitants gut play a crucial role in cholesterol balance and absorption. The bacteria have the capability to impact blood lipid composition through the production of bile acids, coprostanol, SCFAs and TMAO thus influencing heart condition.

HDL or good cholesterol absorbs cholesterol in the blood and carries it back to the liver to flush out. Signalling molecules produced by the bacteria impact this reverse cholesterol transport, a vital protective function.

Microbiota-derived metabolites are either beneficial like SCFA or detrimental like TMAO and can activate several downstream signalling pathways. The dysbiosis-associated breakdown of the gut barrier can trigger elevated blood pressure mechanisms.

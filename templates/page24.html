Bacteria in the gut microbiome are involved in the regulation of hormones and metabolites which are known to play a role in bone health such as Vitamin D, and in the absorption of minerals like Calcium and Magnesium. This leads to certain bacteria being associated with the mineral density of bones and conditions like osteoporosis.

Gut bacteria play a role in maintaining levels of proteins in the body, through both metabolism and by immune regulation. Dysbiosis of the gut microbiome may lead to buildup of harmful proteins in the macula of eyes with age. Which in turn may cause damage to it.

Short chain fatty acids and other metabolites produced by the gut bacteria can interact with receptors in the host to regulate their blood pressure. Controlling these bacteria can help control hypertension with age.

BACTERIA PLAY IN PRODUCING & PROCESSING B12

Our gut also harbours bacteria that produce B vitamins, including biotin, cobalamin, folate, et al, in limited amounts. Several gut bacteria also require vitamins to perform their own functions. Deficiencies of vitamins thus can instigate multiple seemingly unrelated diseases.

This vitamin is vital for cell survival and growth, regulating gene activity, and brain function, among other life functions. There are several bacteria that can produce folate in the gut while others help synthesis.

There are few bacteria that can synthesise Vitamin B12 in your gut. About 20% of gut bacteria can produce Vit B12 and 80% need it for their metabolic reactions.

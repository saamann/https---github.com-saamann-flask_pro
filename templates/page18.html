Depression, a persistent feeling of sadness and a loss of interest in life can be overwhelming. Research shows that those battling mood challenges have a distinct microbiome with an increased leaning towards inflammation and bowel issues

The gut microbiome runs a two-way communication with your brain. These signals can often be over-read or misrecognised. Alternatively, regulating the gut can improve gut-brain communication and alleviate anxiety.

Stress often promotes the consumption of irregular food while sending distress signals to the gut. This ends up defining which bacteria thrive. In turn, the bacteria release metabolites or chemicals that further push up stress and trigger inflammation and autonomic changes.

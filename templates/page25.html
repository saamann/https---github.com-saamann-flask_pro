Bacteria influence immune response which in turn are involved in DNA damage and repair pathways. Furthermore, microbes also produce and consume DNA-damaging metabolites. The accumulation of DNA damage is the primary cause of cancer.

The microbiome influences nutrient sensing through metabolite production and modulation of gut hormones, impacting metabolism, appetite, and energy balance. The loss of the ability to sense nutrients with age leads to many complications.

Bacteria modulate inflammation through immune responses, producing or suppressing inflammatory molecules, impacting overall health and disease susceptibility. The accumulation of inflammation over time with age in turn causes other damage at a cellular level.

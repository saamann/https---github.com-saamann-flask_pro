BACTERIA THAT PLAY A ROLE IN REPEATED INFLAMMATION

A disturbed gut-lung connection not just reduces microbial diversity, but also increases the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines(immune metabolites), resulting in poor clearance of infection in the lungs and or gastrointestinal symptoms.

RTIs can be caused by bacterial, viral or fungal pathogens. And these are not limited to the respiratory tract-they can result in gastrointestinal symptoms like diarrhoea or cramps. Studies show the depletion of several bacteria in those with recurrent RTI.

A healthy lung and gut microbial composition is characterised by an abundance of certain bacterial phyla. An imbalance supports the activation of inflammatory pathways that urge bronchial constriction, and bronchial hyper-responsiveness due to hyperactivity to respiratory and food allergens.

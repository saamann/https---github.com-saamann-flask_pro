Which bacteria are impacting your respiration

The gut and the lung are anatomically distinct. Yet emerging research highlights the impact of the complicated pathways and communications between these organs. The gut-lung axis can not just influence each other but directly impact the course of disease as well as treatment. Changes in the microbiome due to diet, illness or medical intervention are linked with alterations in immune response and balance in the airways. Management strategies for repeated or chronic respiratory challenges thus need to be multidisciplinary to effectively manage symptoms and progression.

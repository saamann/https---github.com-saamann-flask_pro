WHICH BACTERIA ARE IMPACTING YOUR GUT

The gut microbiome is possibly a mediator between metabolism, weight and health outcomes. Multiple studies have shown that increased weight loss is associated with increased statistically significant diversity of the microbiome. Obesity, systemic inflammation, and insulin resistance are associated with lower microbiota diversity and higher intestinal permeability. The microbes influence and steer energy metabolism by regulating glucose metabolism, appetite and fat storage. The microbiome influences both sides of the energy equation-extraction of energy on one side and storage and expenditure of energy on the other. When energy intake consistently exceeds energy expenditure, this excess fat storage can lead to obesity and associated health problems.
